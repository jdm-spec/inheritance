Farāʾiḍ

What is Islamic
Inheritance?

Farāʾiḍ (فرائض) is the divinely legislated system of inheritance in Islam. Unlike man-made inheritance laws, it is rooted in direct Quranic revelation — ensuring justice, protecting family ties, and distributing wealth equitably across generations.

لِلرِّجَالِ نَصِيبٌ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ الْوَالِدَانِ وَالْأَقْرَبُونَ وَلِلنِّسَاءِ نَصِيبٌ مِّمَّا تَرَكَ الْوَالِدَانِ وَالْأَقْرَبُونَ

"For men is a share of what the parents and close relatives leave, and for women is a share of what the parents and close relatives leave..."

— Surah An-Nisa 4:7

The science of inheritance (ʿIlm al-Farāʾiḍ) is considered by scholars to be one of the most important branches of Islamic jurisprudence. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Learn the Farāʾiḍ and teach it to the people, for it is half of knowledge."
— Sunan Ibn Majah

  • 📖

    Quranic Foundation

    Inheritance shares are explicitly defined in the Quran (4:11–12, 4:176) — not left to discretion. These fixed shares (Aṣḥāb al-Furūḍ) cannot be altered.

  • ⚖️

    Fixed Shares (Furūḍ)

    Certain heirs receive fixed fractions: ½, ⅓, ¼, ⅙, ⅛, or ⅔ of the estate depending on surviving relatives.

  • 🔗

    Residuary Heirs (ʿAṣabāt)

    After fixed shares are distributed, the remainder goes to residuary heirs — primarily male agnatic relatives — in order of priority.

  • 🚫

    Conditions of Inheritance

    An heir must be alive at the time of death, Muslim, and not guilty of killing the deceased. Non-Muslim relatives do not inherit from Muslim estates.

  • 🏛️

    Bayt al-Māl

    If no valid heirs exist, the estate passes to the Islamic treasury (Bayt al-Māl) for community benefit — no wealth is left without a rightful destination.

Guide

How to Use
Al-Qismah

Follow these simple steps to calculate the correct Islamic inheritance distribution for any estate — in minutes.

1

Select the Deceased's Gender

Choose whether the deceased was male or female. This affects the spouse's share — a husband receives a different fraction than a wife.

2

Enter Estate Details

Enter the total money amount (₹) and/or property details. For land, enter the quantity, unit (acre/cent), conversion rate, and market rate per unit.

💡 e.g. 2 acre = 200 cent × ₹1,00,000 = ₹2 Cr
3

Add Heirs

Enter the number of each heir type (sons, daughters, wives, etc.). Checkbox heirs like father, mother, grandfather are toggled on/off.

4

Assign Money or Property

For each individual heir, choose whether they receive Money, Property, or Both. This allows flexible real-world distribution.

💡 Son 1 → Money, Son 2 → Property
5

Calculate & Share

Tap Calculate Now. Results show each heir's fraction, percentage, amount, and property equivalent. Share via WhatsApp, print, or copy to clipboard.
